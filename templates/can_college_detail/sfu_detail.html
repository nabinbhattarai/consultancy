📘 About Simon Fraser University (SFU)

Simon Fraser University (SFU), located in British Columbia with campuses in Burnaby, Surrey, and Vancouver, is a leading Canadian institution recognized for its academic excellence, innovative research, and strong community engagement. Established in 1965, SFU offers a wide range of undergraduate and graduate programs across disciplines such as business, engineering, health sciences, environment, arts, and technology. Known for its commitment to sustainability and experiential learning, the university provides students with opportunities to gain practical experience through co-op programs, internships, and research projects. Its modern facilities, diverse student body, and strong industry connections make SFU a dynamic and globally focused university in one of Canada’s most beautiful provinces.

🏫 Campus Life

Campus life at Simon Fraser University is vibrant, diverse, and enriched by its unique location across three campuses in Burnaby, Surrey, and Vancouver. The Burnaby campus, set on Burnaby Mountain, offers stunning views and a close-knit community atmosphere, while the urban Surrey and Vancouver campuses provide direct access to the cultural and professional opportunities of the city. Students can join a wide variety of clubs, cultural groups, and recreational activities that cater to different interests, from sports and arts to academic and social initiatives. The university also places strong emphasis on student wellbeing, offering support services, career resources, and wellness programs. With regular events, festivals, and access to nearby outdoor adventures like hiking and skiing, SFU provides a balanced university experience that blends academics, social engagement, and the natural beauty of British Columbia.
